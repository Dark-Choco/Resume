What Shaped Me

These are the things that influenced me throughout my life.

Neon Genesis Evangelion

Evangelion taught me that peple can never fully understand each other, and that we all hurt each other in some way because of that. But the point isn't to avoid people because of that, but to choose to be with them anyway even knowing that.

As a person who was between in both American and Korean cultures, this anime series helped me go though my wandering phase.

Watch on Netflix

Capernaum (2018)

This movie is about a kid who takes his parents to court for bringing him into the world. It sounds strange but it makes sense once you watch it. It didn't try to make things feel okay by the end. It just showed life as it was for that kid. I sat with it for a while after it was over. That's the kind of impact I want my work to have someday.

Watch on IMDb

Fullmetal Alchemist

FMA introduced me to the idea of equivalent exchange — you can't get something without giving something up. The Elric brothers spend the whole series trying to fix a mistake they made, but the real lesson wasn't about alchemy at all.

The more Ed tried to solve everything through alchemy, the further he got from the truth. The series made me realize that always chasing something more perfect, always wanting more than what you have, actually takes you further from what really matters. The answer was simpler than that — taking care of the people around you, leaning on each other, and learning to be okay with what you already have.

Ed only figured that out by actually going out into the world and meeting people. Not by studying harder or becoming more powerful, but through experience. That kind of growth stuck with me.

Watch on Crunchyroll

RATSHAKER

RATSHAKER is a small, weird game that somehow gets under your skin. It doesn't look expensive or polished, but that's kind of the point. It showed me that a game doesn't need a big team or a big budget to leave an impression. Sometimes the rough edges are what make it feel real.

See it on Steam

Buckshot Roulette

Buckshot Roulette has one mechanic and it works really well. You're just sitting across from someone, taking turns, not knowing what's going to happen. The simplicity is what makes it tense. It taught me that you don't need a complicated system to make a player feel something. You just need the right rules.

See it on Steam
